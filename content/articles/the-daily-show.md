**The Daily Show** is a late-night news satire program. **[[anthony-derosa|Anthony DeRosa]]** spent five years there as Digital Production Manager, joining a digital team led by Baratunde Thurston and helping build it from the ground up. The team won an **[[emmy-award|Emmy Award]]** in 2017 for "Between the Scenes" — the first Emmy of the Trevor Noah era — and received further nominations in 2018 and 2019.

## Trump Twitter Library

DeRosa helped produce the *New York Times* bestseller **[[trump-twitter-library|The Donald J. Trump Presidential Twitter Library]]** and its installations across the country, which won a Cannes Lions Grand Prix and a Clio Award.

## Technical projects

DeRosa's technical work at The Daily Show included a Facebook data scraper built in Ruby, a Twitter bot built in Django, a text randomizer in JavaScript, Chrome extensions built with Bootstrap, React, and JavaScript, and Twilio-powered phone trees including a "Trump Advice Hotline" and the "Trump Perfect Call" line.
