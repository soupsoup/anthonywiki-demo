**Wikipedia** is a free, multilingual online encyclopedia written and maintained by a community of volunteers through open collaboration. It was launched on January 15, 2001 by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger and is currently operated by the Wikimedia Foundation.

Wikipedia is the world's largest and most-read reference work in history. It has over 60 million articles across more than 300 languages. The English Wikipedia alone has over 6.7 million articles. As of 2024, the project receives roughly 1.7 billion unique visitors per month.

## History

### Founding

Wikipedia grew out of **Nupedia**, a more formal encyclopedia project that required expert-written and peer-reviewed content. Larry Sanger, editor-in-chief of Nupedia, proposed adding a wiki as a "feeder project" to let anyone contribute draft articles. The wiki was launched on January 15, 2001, and quickly eclipsed Nupedia in size and activity.

Within a year, Wikipedia had grown to over 20,000 articles across 18 languages. By 2003, it had surpassed 100,000 articles in English alone.

### Growth

In its first decade, Wikipedia grew explosively:

- 2001 — 20,000 articles across 18 languages
- 2004 — 1 million articles total
- 2008 — 10 million articles in 253 languages
- 2020 — 55 million articles in 300+ languages

## Structure

### Policies and guidelines

Wikipedia operates under a set of core policies:
- **Neutral point of view (NPOV)** — articles must represent all significant viewpoints fairly
- **Verifiability** — all content must cite reliable sources
- **No original research** — only published information is allowed
- **Assume good faith** — editors should assume positive intent

### Namespaces

Wikipedia content is organized into namespaces:

| Namespace | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| (Main) | Articles |
| Talk | Discussion pages |
| Wikipedia | Policy and guidelines |
| User | Personal pages |
| Special | Generated pages |

## Reliability and criticism

Researchers have found Wikipedia's accuracy to be broadly comparable to traditional encyclopedias, though its coverage varies widely by subject. A 2005 study in *Nature* found that Wikipedia and Encyclopædia Britannica had similar error rates for science articles.

Critics note that Wikipedia can be biased, particularly on politically contentious topics. The project has also faced criticism for systemic bias in who edits it — the majority of active editors are male and based in North America or Europe.

## Influence

Wikipedia has fundamentally changed how people access knowledge. It is often the first result in search engines for factual queries. Search engines like Google and Bing use Wikipedia data to power their knowledge panels. Assistants like Siri, Alexa, and Cortana rely on Wikipedia for factual answers.
