**SBI** and **GROW** are two complementary frameworks for leadership conversations. SBI structures the delivery of feedback. GROW structures the coaching conversation that follows. Used together, they move from "here's what happened" to "here's what changes."

## SBI Model — for giving feedback

SBI stands for **Situation, Behavior, Impact**. The structure keeps feedback observable and non-judgmental.

**S — Situation**: Describe the specific context where the behavior occurred. Not "lately" or "sometimes" — a concrete time and place.

**B — Behavior**: Describe what you observed, not what you inferred or assumed about motive. Observable actions only.

**I — Impact**: Explain the effect on outcomes, the team, or stakeholders.

**Example from the training session:**
> Someone repeatedly interrupted a teammate in a meeting.
> - *Situation*: "In yesterday's team meeting…"
> - *Behavior*: "…I noticed you interrupted Ben a few times while he was presenting, cutting off his points."
> - *Impact*: "…After that, others stopped sharing and contributing."

The SBI structure sets up a coaching conversation without triggering defensiveness — it describes facts, not character.

## GROW Model — for coaching

GROW stands for **Goal, Reality, Options, Will**. It follows naturally after SBI feedback, shifting from description to development.

**G — Goal**: What does the person want their behavior or presence to look like? ("What do you want your leadership presence in meetings to look like?")

**R — Reality**: How did it actually land? ("How do you think that meeting landed with the team?")

**O — Options**: What are different ways to change the behavior? ("What are some ways you could make more space for others to contribute?")

**W — Will**: What will they do, and how will they follow through? Specific commitment, not vague intent.

The research-backed argument for GROW: people act more reliably on solutions they generate themselves than on ones prescribed to them. The coach's job is to ask better questions, not give better answers.

## Putting them together

SBI delivers the observation without blame. GROW builds the path forward without prescription. The sequence:
1. Use SBI to describe what happened and its impact
2. Use GROW to guide the person toward their own solution
3. End with a specific "Will" — what they're committing to try

## Related practices from the same session

- **Separate coaching from status meetings**: Use weekly 1:1s for blockers and progress, separate monthly or quarterly sessions for development conversations
- **Stay interview questions**: "What keeps you here? What would make you leave?" Run these before people are out the door
- **Three-question development structure**: What do you love about your job? What do you want more of? What do you want less of?
- **Reverse feedback**: Ask your direct reports for feedback on you in the same 1:1 you use for coaching them
